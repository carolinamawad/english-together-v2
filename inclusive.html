Teach Inclusively

Inclusive teaching means creating lessons where every learner—child, teenager, or adult—feels valued, supported, and engaged. This section offers strategies, resources, and examples to help future teachers design lessons that embrace diversity and promote equity.

Inclusive Classroom Image

Key Inclusive Strategies

  • Use clear instructions tailored to different learning needs.
  • Offer multiple ways to participate (speaking, drawing, movement).
  • Adapt materials for students with diverse abilities.
  • Encourage collaboration in mixed-skill groups.
  • Provide emotional support and create a safe learning environment.

Practical Examples for ELT

  • Visual schedules for young learners.
  • Sentence starters for beginners.
  • Subtitles and transcripts for listening activities.
  • Choice boards where students select activities.
  • Accessible reading texts with images and keywords.
