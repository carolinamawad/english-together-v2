Teach Inclusively

Inclusive teaching means creating lessons where every learner—child, teenager, or adult—feels valued, supported, and engaged. This section offers strategies, resources, and examples to help future teachers design lessons that embrace diversity and promote equity.

Inclusive Classroom

Key Inclusive Strategies

  • Clear Instructions: Use simple and accessible directions for all learners.
  • Multiple Participation Options: Allow students to express understanding through speaking, writing, drawing, or movement.
  • Adapted Materials: Provide alternatives for students with different needs.
  • Collaborative Learning: Encourage teamwork in mixed-ability groups.
  • Emotional Safety: Promote respect, kindness, and a supportive environment.

Practical Examples for ELT

  • Visual schedules for young learners.
  • Sentence starters to support beginners.
  • Listening activities with transcripts and subtitles.
  • Choice boards that allow students to select activities.
  • Accessible texts with images and highlighted keywords.
